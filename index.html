📥 Import Blog Post from Google Doc
How it works: Format your Google Doc using the template labels (see "View Template Format" tab), then copy the entire doc and paste it below. The builder will automatically fill every field — title, all sections, opening, closing, and CTA.

Labels look like [TITLE], [SECTION 1 HEADING], [SECTION 2 BODY], etc. To add extra paragraph blocks use [SECTION 1 PARA 1], [SECTION 1 PARA 2], and so on. Anything without a label is ignored.
Copy this template into your Google Doc. Replace the placeholder text with your blog content. Use as many [SECTION N] blocks as you need (Section 1, 2, 3…). Each section can optionally have a [SECTION N TIP] and [SECTION N QUOTE].
[TITLE] Your Blog Post Title Here [TITLE_EMPHASIS] Words to highlight in orange (must appear in title) [AUTHOR] The Ceries Team [DATE] April 2026 [OPENING] Your opening / intro paragraph goes here. Write as many paragraphs as you want — separate them with a blank line. [SECTION 1 HEADING] First Section Subheading [SECTION 1 BODY] Body text for section 1. Separate paragraphs with a blank line. [SECTION 1 TIP] Optional quick tip for section 1. [SECTION 1 QUOTE] Optional pull quote for section 1. [SECTION 2 HEADING] Second Section Subheading [SECTION 2 BODY] Body text for section 2. [SECTION 3 HEADING] Third Section Subheading [SECTION 3 BODY] Body text for section 3. [CLOSING HEADING] Wrapping Up [CLOSING BODY] Your closing paragraph here. [CTA EYEBROW] Ready to scale your fulfillment? [CTA HEADLINE] Let Ceries Handle the Heavy Lifting. [CTA BODY] Short 1-2 sentence call to action.
